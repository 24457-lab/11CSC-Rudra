Adaptation Strategies

New Zealand uses several adaptation strategies to reduce the impacts of rising sea levels. These approaches help protect communities, infrastructure and natural environments.

🛡 Coastal Protection

🏗 Seawalls and Revetments

Physical barriers that protect coastal land from wave damage and erosion.

🏖 Beach Nourishment

Adding sand to beaches to replace material lost through erosion.

⚓ Breakwaters and Groynes

Structures that reduce wave energy and protect shorelines.

🚧 Flood Gates and Barriers

Used to reduce flooding during storm surges and extreme weather events.

🏠 Managed Retreat

📊 Risk Assessment

Identifying areas most vulnerable to future sea level rise.

👥 Community Planning

Working with residents to create long-term adaptation plans.

📅 Phased Relocation

Moving people and infrastructure gradually from high-risk locations.

🌳 Land Use Changes

Converting vulnerable land into safer and more sustainable uses.
